## Quick Reference Guide

1. Halt the Flight before entering Hazard Area
2. Move forward, post in front of flight at sidewalk/hazard edge
3. Wave traffic to pass OR Motion traffic to stop
	1. Extend *either* arm, display palm of hand with fingers pointed upward
4. March into intersection/parking lot, perform about face to face the flight
	 "Road Guards, **OUT**"
	1. Road Guards go to lane directed by Flight Leader
	2. Assume Stop Position
		1. If carrying flashlight, hold flashlight extended with beam pointed **DOWN**
5. Command flight to march across intersection, performing necessary facing movements to observe
6. Halt flight once across
	"Road Guards, **IN**"
	1. Road Gaurds return to rear of flight, while FL remains in intersection
7. FL continues commands **ONLY AFTER** rejoining flight.

---
*From OTS SPINS 2.4.2*

1. For safety reasons, flights will always be halted prior to entering a hazard area so the Flight Leader can ensure the safety of the posting road guards. Hazard areas include all intersections where vehicular transit occurs including parking lots.
   
2. The Flight Leader will move forward, post in front of the flight at the sidewalk or hazard edge.
   
3. The Flight Leader will wave traffic past OR motion for the traffic to stop by extending either arm and displaying the palm of the hand with the fingers pointed upward.
4. The Flight Leader will then march into the intersection and post in the middle of the road, perform an about face (so they are facing the flight), ensure traffic is stopped and command, “Road guards, OUT.”
5. On the command, “Road guards, OUT,” each road guard will go to the lane as directed by the Flight Leader. They will face oncoming traffic at modified parade rest with the right arm up and palm flat with the fingers pointed upward. If carrying a flashlight, road guards will hold the flashlight in the extended arm with the beam pointed down.
6. The Flight Leader will command the flight to march across the intersection and perform the necessary facing movements so they can always observe the flight.
7. Once the flight is through the intersection, the Flight Leader will halt the flight and command, “Road guards, IN.”
8. The Flight Leader will remain in the intersection and continue to halt existing traffic while the road guards return to the rear of the flight.
9. The Flight Leader does not give further commands (e.g., “Forward, MARCH”) until rejoining the flight.
10. Road guards or formation leaders will report vehicles that fail to observe safety precautions or follow road guard directions. Officer Trainees will report the time, place, type of vehicle, license number (if known), and description of the violation to their instructor.
11. When two or more formations approach opposite ends of a hazard, the formation proceeding to an appointment has priority and the returning formation must yield. The returning formation must move to the side opposite of the hazard and out of the proceeding formation’s path. Once the formation with priority has passed, the returning formation will resume transit on the designated route and execute all road guard procedures as needed.
